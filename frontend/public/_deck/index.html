A6·Appendix · what Taali measures

The five Ds turn AI use into hiring evidence you can defend.

Every dimension answers a practical question about how the candidate works with AI — and every score links back to the session behind it.

01 · Delegation

Delegation

Do they choose what to own, what to hand to AI, and what must stay human?

Strong candidates keep judgement and design; they delegate the mechanical work.

Evidence · task split + prompts
02 · Description

Description

Do they give AI clear goals, context, constraints and useful feedback?

Vague prompts produce vague work. Clear direction is a hiring signal in itself.

Evidence · prompt quality
03 · Discernment

Discernment

Do they challenge output, spot errors and select what is safe and useful?

The difference between using AI and being used by it. The core of the signal.

Evidence · edits + corrections
04 · Diligence

Diligence

Do they test, verify and manage the residual risk instead of trusting blindly?

Validation runs, tests and checks show who ships work they can stand behind.

Evidence · tests + verification
05 · Deliverable

Deliverable

Is the final result correct, high quality — and does it meet the brief?

Process matters, but the work still has to be good. The outcome is scored too.

Evidence · final deliverable
Prompt + tool trace→Edits + tests→Final deliverable→Evidence-linked report + interview questions

A7·Appendix · data protection & accountable automation

Compliant, accountable hiring — human oversight and traceable decisions.

Taali operates in line with UK and EU data-protection requirements — practical product controls, paired with a documented compliance process with every customer.

Product controls today

Control who acts, what runs and what leaves the system.

01Tenant-scoped access, with per-role permissions for edits, agent controls and team management.
02Role-level autonomy and review holds for out-of-rules or low-confidence actions.
03Exportable decision history: reasoning, evidence, versions and the human resolution.
04Separate client-safe views with time-limited, revocable or single-view links — plus data-access and erasure workflows.
Documented with each customer

The lawful process around the product.

01Controller/processor roles, purpose, lawful basis and the candidate privacy notice.
02DPIA, retention/deletion schedule and data-subject request map.
03Candidate explanation, human-intervention and contest route.
04DPA, subprocessors, transfer mechanisms and EU AI Act readiness plan.
Regulatory frame: UK GDPR + DPA 2018 as amended by the Data (Use and Access) Act 2025. EU recruitment AI is a high-risk category; the current employment-system timetable is 2 December 2027. ICO guidance ↗ · EU AI Act timetable ↗
